# 🛒 Superstore Sales Performance Dashboard

**Week 2 of my One Project Per Week Resolution**

## 📋 Project Overview
Analysis of 9,994 orders from a US-based superstore 
covering 4 years of sales data across 4 regions, 
3 product categories and 17 sub-categories.

**Business Question:** Which products, regions and 
customers are driving revenue — and which are 
underperforming?

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| Overall profit margin | 12.47% — below 20% industry average |
| Best region | West — $108K profit, 14.9% margin |
| Worst region | Central — only 7.9% margin |
| Best category | Technology — 17.4% margin |
| Worst category | Furniture — only 2.49% margin |
| Biggest loss maker | Tables — losing $17,725 |
| Peak sales month | November every year |
| Biggest problem state | Texas — losing $25,729 |
| Discount threshold | Above 20% always loses money |

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

**1. Stop heavy discounting on Tables**
Reduce average discount from 26% to maximum 10%.
Tables are losing $17,725 — the more they sell 
the more money the business loses.

**2. Review Central region strategy**
Texas and Illinois alone are losing $38,337 combined.
Investigate if heavy discounting in these states 
is the root cause.

**3. Push Technology products**
Technology has the highest profit margin at 17.4%.
The business should prioritise Technology in all 
marketing and sales efforts.

**4. Reward zero-discount customers**
Customers like Hunter Lopez and Sanjit Chand buy 
without any discount and are highly profitable.
Create a VIP programme to retain them.

**5. Prepare for November peak season**
November is consistently the highest revenue month 
due to Black Friday and Christmas shopping.
Stock up inventory by October every year.
